# Key Characters

## DCI Thomas Nightingale

**Senior magical practitioner and officer in charge of the Folly.**

A wizard who looks remarkably young for a man born around 1900. Since the 1960s, he has been getting younger — no one knows exactly how. He stands about 180 cm tall with fine features, grey eyes, and short brown hair.

He's always well-dressed in a suit, carrying a silver-topped cane that doubles as his wizard's staff. He speaks with Received Pronunciation ("the Queen's English") and comes from an affluent, upper-middle-class background.

**Background:** Educated at Casterbrook, a now-defunct boarding school for young wizards. He spent the interwar period trouble-shooting for the Foreign Office and Colonial Office. During World War II, he fought German magical forces in occupied Europe and led the rear-guard action during Operation Spatchcock — the British attempt to capture Nazi magical research at Ettersberg Camp.

**Known abilities:** One of the most powerful Newtonian practitioners in Britain. His silver-topped walking cane contains 60 magic points.

**Personality:** Dryly humorous, deeply experienced, cares about his team. He enjoys watching rugby and drives a classic grey Jaguar Mark 2.

## Peter Grant

**DCI and Nightingale's apprentice, Newtonian practitioner.**

A PC who was promoted to DCI — unusual for someone his age and class. Born on the Peckwater Estate, working class, with a London accent. His mother is a cleaner and his father a jazz musician. He lacks a university degree.

**Background:** Recruited by Nightingale in January 2012 after encountering a ghost. He became Nightingale's first apprentice and helped the Folly expand beyond a one-person operation.

**Personality:** Grounded, practical, uses humour to defuse tension. He's the reader's eyes into the magical world — often the most "normal" character in extraordinary situations.

---

## Molly

**Folly housekeeper, fae.**

A small, slim woman dressed like an Edwardian maid in a black skirt, white bib, and white cotton blouse. She has very pale skin, black almond-shaped eyes, a long narrow face, and long black hair. She's looked the same for at least 70 years. She never speaks but can understand English and occasionally hisses.

**Abilities:**
- **Haemomancy** (divination through blood) — If she feeds on a person's blood, she can increase their sensitivity to vestigia, extending it outward by several miles.
- **Luck of the Demi-monde** — Her Luck value equals her POW.
- **Blood affinity** — Her thematic power relates to blood magic.

**Background:** Rescued from a stage magician in 1911 by Nightingale. She has remained at the Folly of her own volition ever since.

**Note:** Molly has a strong bond with Nightingale and Foxglove. She is very fond of Toby (the Folly's dog). Her basement quarters are private and off the building plans — outsiders are not to poke around down there.

## Foxglove Summer

**High Fae artist.**

A tall, slender fae woman with elongated arms and long-fingered hands. Her face is long and oval with high, prominent cheekbones, a small mouth and chin, and large hazel eyes with flecks of silver and gold. Like Molly, she has far too many teeth. She tends to wear loose artist's smocks spattered with paint.

**Abilities:**
- **Magical nullification** — Her presence creates a field that negates magic. This is why her studio was deliberately placed in the basement custody suite.
- **Luck of the Demi-monde** — Her Luck value equals her POW.

**Background:** Separated from the fae decades ago after being sold by a High Fae queen. She was exploited in London clubs before being isolated in a pit for 15 years. She escaped alongside Nightingale and came to the Folly.

**Personality:** Expresses herself through art rather than speech. Close friends with Molly. The modern world is something of a mystery to her.

---

## Nafeesa Jones

**Police Officer and Newtonian practitioner.**

A recent recruit to the London Metropolitan Police Service and an example investigator. She has high Strength (70) and Intelligence (60), with a Dexterity of 40 — she's a bit clumsy. She took the **Magical** advantage, allowing her to cast spells.

**Background:** She's the example character used in the rulebook to illustrate character creation and rules. She's a straight-arrow, determined police officer who is learning magic.

---

## Dr Abdul Haqq Walid

**Forensic pathologist at the Iain West Forensic Suite.**

Handles "special" cases at Westminster Public Mortuary, including cryptopathology (investigating deaths that involve magical elements).

---

## Dr Jennifer Vaughan

**Medical professional at University College Hospital.**

Also handles special cases involving magic and the supernatural.

---

## Abigail Kamara

**DC and DC Grant's half-cousin.**

A contact who has had significant experience with magical phenomena. She is more informed than she initially appears and has prior connections to the Folly context.

---

## Sgt Jaget Kumar

**British Transport Police liaison.**

Responsible for keeping track of "weird shit" on the London Underground. He has unofficially become the Folly's contact for supernatural incidents involving the Tube and railways.

---

## The Quiet People

**Non-human entities living underground.**

A group that has been "born different" and lives in a strange subterranean community below London. They are associated with earth-based powers and have connections to the London Underground. They are not ghosts or demi-monde beings in the traditional sense — something entirely different.

---

## River Spirits (Genii Loci)

London's rivers each have a spirit. Nightingale has known **Father Thames** (the spirit of the River Thames) for a long time. River spirits are powerful demi-monde beings with deep connections to their waterways.
