# London — The Setting

## When and Where

The game is set in **London in 2016**, shortly after the events of the novel *False Value*. The city is both recognisably real and layered with a hidden magical world.

You don't need to have read any of Ben Aaronovitch's novels to enjoy the game — everything you need is contained within the rulebook. Reading the novels is a great way to expand your knowledge once you've started playing.

## The British Class System

The world of Rivers of London is firmly rooted in the **British class system**. This affects how people are treated, what opportunities they have, and how they interact.

The class system has three main levels:

1. **Working Class** — Growing up in council estates, no university degree, often with a strong accent. Example: Peter Grant, who grew up on the Peckwater Estate in Peckworth with his mother as a cleaner and father as a jazz musician.
2. **Middle Class** — University education, professional career, Received Pronunciation. Example: DCI Nightingale, who comes from an affluent family and was privately educated.
3. **Upper Class** — Old money, elite education (Eton, Harrow), deep social networks.

Even today, social elites form networks to support and promote each other at the expense of those from lower classes — the so-called "Old Boy Networks." Being from a particular class can affect how people treat you, above and beyond gender and ethnicity.

## Key Police Locations

### Charing Cross Police Station

Located in Covent Garden. Young officers from CX are regularly on the beat around Covent Garden. This is where then-PCs Grant and May first encountered the deceased actor Nicholas Wallpenny.

### Belgravia Police Station

Buckingham Palace Road. Home to the Westminster Murder Team and DCI Alexander Seawoll.

### Kentish Town Police Station

Holmes Road, close to Hampstead Heath. Contact: DC Karen Jonquiere (Kay), who has special training in interviewing children and teens.

### Southwark Police Station

Borough High Street. Contact: Sergeant William Daverc, who liaised with the Folly in the past.

### New Scotland Yard

The Commissioner of the Metropolitan Police Service is based there and is fully informed about the Folly's workings. They have authority over Met police operations. The Deputy Assistant Commissioner, Richard Folsom, has ties to the demi-monde.

### Empress State Building (ESB)

West Brompton, West London. Houses high-level meeting rooms, Counter Terrorism Command space, and the Directorate of Professional Standards (DPS; Police Oversight). Also an assessment centre for prospective police officers.

### Iain West Forensic Suite

Westminster Public Mortuary on Horseferry Road. Used for post-mortem examinations. Dr Abdul Haqq Walid and Dr Jennifer Vaughan handle "special" cases including cryptopathology.

## Key London Locations

### Stonewaters Bookshop

New Row, Covent Garden. A listed Georgian building with a basement where supernatural incidents can occur.

### Regent's Canal

A key geographic feature running through North London. Ley lines and supernatural phenomena can be traced along canal corridors.

### Skygarden

An East Walworth tower block investigated during cases involving drug dealing and supernatural elements.

### The Ettersberg Camp

A site in Germany where British forces attempted to capture Nazi magical research during WWII. The research — known as "The Black Library" — was brought back to London and is now stashed in a secure vault beneath the Folly.

## The Thames and River Spirits

The River Thames has a spirit known as **Father Thames** — a powerful genii locorum (spirit of a place). The Thames and its tributaries are central to the lore, with each river having its own spirit. Rivers store vast amounts of magic and vestigia.

## The London Underground

The Tube has more than its fair share of weirdness, including ghosts and the Quiet People (non-human entities living underground). British Transport Police's contact for supernatural incidents is Sgt Jaget Kumar.

## A Note on London as a Character

London in the Rivers of London universe is almost a character itself — it's old, layered with history, full of hidden places and secret passages. The city stores magic in its stone, and this magic is what powers ghosts and vestigia. The older the building, the more magic it can hold.
