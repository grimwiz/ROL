# Glossary of Terms

A quick-reference guide to key terms used in the Rivers of London game.

## General Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Case file** | A scenario or adventure in the game |
| **Campaign** | A series of connected case files |
| **The Folly** | Secret branch of the Met police that handles supernatural crime |
| **DCI** | Detective Chief Inspector |
| **PC** | Police Constable (junior police rank) |
| **POW** | Power — a character characteristic representing willpower and magical potential |
| **VESTIGIA** | Traces of magic detectable by practitioners |
| **SIGNARE** | A practitioner's unique magical signature, left when casting spells |
| **MSA** | Magical Suppression Area |
| **HTD** | Hyperthaumaturgical Degradation — magical backlash from overcasting |
| **PACE** | Police and Criminal Evidence Act — UK law governing police procedure |

## Magic Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Newtonian Magic** | Spellcasting system developed by Sir Isaac Newton |
| **Demi-monde** | Non-human magical beings (fae, spirits, etc.) |
| **Glamour** | The power to sway someone's mind (a demi-monde ability) |
| **Thematic Power** | A demi-monde's sphere of influence granting them magical abilities |
| **Genius Loci** | Spirit of a place (e.g., river spirit) |
| **Genii Loci** | Plural of genius loci |
| **Wizard's Staff** | A physical conduit for magic, storing magic points |
| **Formae** | The building blocks of Newtonian spells |
| **Order (of a spell)** | The base cost in magic points to cast a spell |
| **First Order** | Basic spells (Aqua, Impello, etc.) |
| **Second Order** | More advanced spells (Candano, Fireball, etc.) |

## Being Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Fae** | A type of demi-monde being; not to be confused with High Fae |
| **High Fae** | Tall, elegant demi-monde beings with affinities to nature |
| **Ghosts** | Imprints of personality left after death; powered by stored magic in stone |
| **Loopers** | Ghosts that repeat a short recording |
| **Simulacra** | Ghosts with limited actions and speech |
| **Entities** | Ghosts that appear to act like real people |
| **Revenants** | Spirits out for revenge; can feed on other ghosts |
| **Poltergeist** | A genuinely dangerous supernatural entity; requires 100+ years to develop |
| **Quiet People** | Non-human entities living underground beneath London |
| **River Spirits** | Powerful genii locori associated with London's rivers |

## Locations

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Caxton Street** | The Folly's address in Chelsea |
| **Black Library** | Secure vault beneath the Folly containing captured Nazi magical research |
| **Ettersberg Camp** | German facility where British forces captured Nazi magical research during WWII |
| **Stonewaters** | Bookshop in Covent Garden, site of supernatural incidents |
| **ESB** | Empress State Building — Met offices in West Brompton |

---

*This glossary covers the most commonly-used terms. For more detail, refer to Chapter 5 and Chapter 7 of the rulebook.*
