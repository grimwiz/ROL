# Magic in the Game

This guide covers the two main types of magic you'll encounter and potentially use in the game.

---

## Newtonian Magic

Newtonian magic is the system of spellcasting developed by Sir Isaac Newton. It's the primary magical tradition used by the Folly.

### How It Works

- Spells are cast using **Magic skill rolls** and require **magic points** to fuel them.
- A character's **magic point total** equals one-fifth of their Power (POW) characteristic plus 1 for each spell they have mastered.
- Each spell has a **base cost** equal to its **order** (first order = 1 point, second order = 2 points, etc.).
- Once a practitioner runs out of magic points, they may continue casting but risk harm through **Hyperthaumaturgical Degradation (HTD)**.

### Spellcasting Basics

- **Casting a spell** takes one combat round (or the narrative equivalent) — the same as throwing a punch or firing a gun.
- Outside of combat, spellcasting rolls can be **pushed** (at risk of burning out all magic points).
- Inside combat, spells work like firearms — the Magic roll replaces the Firearms roll, and the damage roll uses DEX.

### Detecting Magic

- **Vestigia** (traces of magic) can be detected using the **Sense Vestigia** skill. Extremely strong vestigia requires no roll; weak vestigia needs a Regular or Hard roll depending on what you want to detect.
- **Signare** (a practitioner's unique magical signature) can also be detected — useful for tracking who cast a spell.

### Hyperthaumaturgical Degradation (HTD)

If you fumble a Magic roll, fail a pushed spellcasting roll, or cast with zero magic points, you must make a **POW roll** for HTD:

| Result | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Critical | Magic points reduced to zero, no damage |
| Regular | Magic points zero, take 2 damage |
| Hard | Magic points zero, take 1 damage |
| Fail | Magic points zero, take 4 damage (Mortal Wound) |
| Fumble | Magic points zero, take 5 damage (fatal blow). Spend 30 Luck immediately or die. |

### Wizard's Staffs

Some practitioners (like Nightingale) carry a **wizard's staff** — a physical conduit for magic that stores a separate pool of magic points (60 for Nightingale's silver-topped cane).

### Spells and Their Classes

Spells are organised into orders:
- **First Order** — Basic spells (Aqua, Impello, Flash Bulb, Werelight)
- **Second Order** — More advanced (Candano/Tanglefoot, Car Killer, Fireball)
- Higher orders exist and are progressively more dangerous

Some spells have **prerequisites** — you must master prerequisite spells first.

## Demi-Monde Magic

Demi-monde magic differs from Newtonian magic. It is used by non-human magical beings (fae, spirits, etc.) and follows different rules.

### The Glamour

The power to sway someone's mind.

**Against unaware targets:**
- **Fumble/Fail** — Target obeys without question and doesn't wonder why
- **Critical** — Target is completely unaffected
- **Regular/Hard** — Target obeys but later wonders what possessed them, rationalising it

**Against informed targets:**
- **Fumble** — Target obeys as if unaware of glamour
- **Regular/Hard/Critical** — Target is aware of the glamour but not compelled
- **Fail** — Target partially obeys (a few steps/words) then recovers control

### Thematic Power

Each demi-monde being has an **affinity** with an element or concept, granting them certain powers.

- **Minor manifestations** — Illustrate their affinity. Colour and character, not plot-driving.
- **Major manifestations** — Furthers the plot or provides an in-game benefit. One per case file. Demanding emotionally and energetically.

### Luck of the Demi-monde

A demi-monde's Luck value equals their POW and **refreshes once every day** (not at the end of a scene like human characters).

## Ghosts

When a person dies, an imprint of their personality can be left on their surroundings. Ghosts gain power from the magic stored in their location — stone is especially good at storing magic.

### Ghost Types

- **Loopers** — Short recordings played on a loop (seconds to hours)
- **Simulacra** — Limited range of actions and speech, repetitive (like computer game characters)
- **Entities** — Appear to act like a real person, may show comprehension

### What People Sometimes Mistake for Ghosts

- **Revenants** — Spirits out for revenge; can feed on ghosts
- **Poltergeists** — Sometimes possessed items or non-corporeal genius loci. It takes at least 100 years for a genuine poltergeist to develop.

### The Annie Scale

Ghosts are graded by intensity on a scale from 1 to 10:
- **1 Annie** — Feeling someone is stood behind you
- **10 Annies** — Appear as real as you or I

Giving a ghost a **werelight** to feed on is the best way to get their attention.

### Important Note

> Ghosts do not have statistics. If something claiming to be a ghost has statistics, it's lying.
