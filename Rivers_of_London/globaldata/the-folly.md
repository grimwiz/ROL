# The Folly

## What Is the Folly?

The Folly is the operational headquarters of the **Special Assessment Unit**, a secret branch of the Metropolitan Police Service that investigates supernatural crime in London. Its official designation is **Newtonian Magic Assessment Unit** — but no one calls it that.

The name "Folly" comes from its location on **Caxton Street, Chelsea, London, SW1**. It's a former **academy for young wizards** built in the early 20th century, named after Sir Isaac Newton, whose coat of arms and motto ("Scientia Potestas Est" — "Knowledge is Power") appear above the front door.

The building was originally a magical academy founded by Newtonian practitioners. After the academy closed, it was converted into a section house, then later repurposed specifically for the Folly's magical investigative work.

## The Folly's Purpose

- Investigates **supernatural crime** in London
- Manages interactions with **the demi-monde** (non-human magical beings)
- Handles **vestigia** (traces of magic) incidents
- Maintains the security of the **Black Library** — a secure vault beneath the building containing dangerous magical research
- Supports police officers seconded to magical cases, including **civilian consultants** sworn in as Folly consultants

## The Building

The Folly is a large, listed Victorian building with multiple floors, each serving different purposes. Because of its age and magical nature, it resists technology — **magic sands the chips** of microprocessors, so there are **no computers inside the main building** (except in the custody suite, most of the time). The library index system is card-based.

The building has **wards** to protect it from external magic, renewed after major incidents.

### Ground Floor

- **Entrance Lobby** — Main entry with a lobby booth and switchboard
- **Atrium** — The grandest room, with pillars, balconies, and a domed glass roof. Also the warmest room in winter
- **Briefing Room** — Former visitor's lounge, now with metal desks and a whiteboard. Portraits of Sir Isaac Newton, Queen Caroline, and the fifth Duke of Bedford hang on the walls
- **Mundane Library** — Non-magical reference collection, card-based index
- **Dining Room & Private Dining Room** — Where meals are prepared and served
- **Lecture Hall** — For teaching and training
- **Storage rooms, WCs**

### First Floor

- **Magical Library** — The heart of the Folly's magical knowledge. Contains books on magic, ghost sightings, and magical research. Has a lecture ladder on brass rails leading to a mezzanine
- **Senior Reading Room** — A quieter space for research
- **Teaching Lab** — Used for spell instruction
- **Studies and bedrooms** for senior practitioners

### Basement

- **Custody Suite** — PACE-compliant holding area with six cells, search room, medical examination suite, interview room, exercise room, and showers
- **Magical Suppression Area (MSA)** — Foxglove's art studio, deliberately placed in the basement because her presence negates magic. This prevents practitioners in cells from casting spells
- **Molly's quarters** — Private space for the housekeeper. Off the plans to protect her privacy
- **Secure Storage** — Where confiscated dangerous magical items are kept. Out of bounds unless DCI Nightingale approves

### Key Spaces Players May Know About

- **The Coach House** — Where investigators park; also a secondary entrance
- **The Portakabin** — Temporary office space
- **Courtyard Gates** — Main vehicle access
- **The Atrium** — Often the centre of social interaction at the Folly

### Secret Passages

Three wall panels off the atrium are actually concealed doors to narrow servant corridors with bare brick walls and single lightbulbs. These were originally built for the working classes who served in the building.
